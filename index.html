About

Hi, I'm Karim Abdul Aziz Chatab — a passionate data engineer, problem solver, and explorer of all things data and technology. I recently graduated from Monash University Malaysia with a major in Business Analytics and Finance, and my career journey has taken me from Melbourne to Kuala Lumpur and the UK, working on projects that blend data engineering, analytics, and business strategy. I've always believed that data isn't just numbers — it's stories waiting to be told. Whether I was building backend pipelines and automating data workflows at GEOMOTION, scraping social media and news sentiment data at ENZEE, or deploying deep learning models on AWS during my training at OESON, my focus has been clear: transform raw data into meaningful, actionable insights. My academic path also reflects this curiosity. Through an exchange at Warwick University in the UK, I dove into topics like Lean Startup and Entrepreneurial Finance Ventures, expanding my perspective on how data-driven ideas fuel modern business innovation. What excites me most is the intersection of data engineering, web scraping, cloud infrastructure, and natural language processing. I thrive on challenges that require not just technical skills, but creative thinking and clear communication. From visualizing data pipelines to leading interns in data storytelling, I aim to make complex systems understandable and impactful. This portfolio is a glimpse into the projects, skills, and ideas I've built along the way. If you're as passionate about data, technology, and innovation as I am — let's connect and create something extraordinary.
